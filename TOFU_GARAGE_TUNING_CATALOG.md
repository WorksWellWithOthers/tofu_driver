# Tofu Garage Tuning Catalog

This catalog is the canonical source material for future Tofu Garage Dream Build and tuning
progression. It is a game design document, not real-world installation advice.

## Product Boundary

`Don't Spill the Cup` is the real-world smooth-driving challenge. Its safety and privacy contract
remains strict: no speed rewards, no public-road competition, no unsafe road incentives, and no raw
GPS, route, speed, or motion uploads.

`Tofu Garage` is a fictional standalone idle, tuning, and garage-management game intended to grow
toward a future standalone Steam/console release. Authentic tuner vocabulary is allowed here:
racing, track-day, slicks, anti-lag, nitrous, turbo, supercharger, drift, brake balance, LSD,
sequential transmission, aero, downforce, and engine swaps can all be game terms.

Boundary rule:

```text
Authentic parts, fictional consequences.
Real vocabulary, game systems.
Garage progression does not alter real-world Cup Test proof.
```

Tofu Garage parts may affect fictional systems such as Garage Build Value, Build Score, Style,
Reliability, Comfort, Control, Grip, Power, Response, Cooling, Durability, Event Fit, Showcase
Readiness, Collector Appeal, Race Class, Garage Reputation, Brand Value, prize potential, and
future car-management outcomes. They must not affect Don't Spill the Cup scoring, certification,
route-context achievements, Perfect Pour proof, Driver XP, real-driving rewards, route validation,
or share proof.

## Category Taxonomy

### Tires & Rubber

Likely game effects: Grip, Comfort, Event Fit, Weather Fit, Race Class, Style, Collector Appeal.

Early use: simple tire identity choices.

Late use: event-specific wet, slick, dirt, R-compound, and weather-fit choices.

Parts:

- Comfort (Hard / Medium / Soft)
- Sports (Hard / Medium / Soft)
- Racing (Hard / Medium / Soft)
- Extreme Performance Summer Tires
- Track-Day R-Compound Tires
- Racing Slicks & Semi-Slicks
- Intermediate
- Full Wet
- Dirt Tires

### Suspension & Chassis Geometry

Likely game effects: Control, Comfort, Grip, Style, Event Fit, Showcase Readiness, Race Class.

Early use: Suspension Refreshed and Ride Height Set.

Late use: coilover tuning, geometry, bracing, and chassis identity.

Parts:

- Sports Suspension (Fixed Springs)
- Height-Adjustable Sports Suspension
- Fully Customizable Suspension (Coilovers)
- Adjustable Coilovers (1-Way, 2-Way, 3-Way, or 4-Way independent damping)
- Fully Customizable Manual Suspension (Active System)
- Adjustable Control Arms / Camber & Toe Arms
- Polyurethane or Solid Aluminum Suspension Bushings
- Upgraded Sway Bars (Anti-Roll Bars) with Adjustable Endlinks
- Roll Center Adjusters (Extended Ball Joints)
- Strut Tower Braces & Underbody X-Braces / Tie Bars

### Brakes & Control

Likely game effects: Control, Reliability, Durability, Event Fit, Race Class, Style.

Late use: brake balance, hydraulic handbrake, and event-specific control builds.

- Sports / Clubsports / Racing Brake Pads

## Current Suspension Track Guidance

Use more authentic language for Suspension while keeping runtime purchases narrow:

- Level 1: Suspension Refreshed. Sports suspension, refreshed bushings, and a basic alignment
  foundation.
- Level 2: Ride Height Set. Height-adjustable sports suspension.
- Level 3: Alignment Dialed. Camber/toe arms and sway-bar setup.
- Level 4: Corner Balance. Fully customizable coilover setup and chassis geometry.
- Level 5: Showcase Stance. Final geometry, bracing, fitment, and presentation.

Suspension Track Completion V1 implements Levels 1-5. Tires & Rubber is the next future build
track and should be pulled from this catalog only when that runtime slice is intentionally scoped.

## Future Runtime Guidance
